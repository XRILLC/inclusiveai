### Project Goals
The goal of this project is to identify the best multimodal LLM for classifying image and (multi-lingual) text based data from Instagram. The data was collected using Selenium and Beautiful soup for my dissertation project. The content of the data is of a Mexican social movement and is therefore multi-lingual as a small portion of the posts are in English, the rest in Spanish.
- A secondary goal would be to turn the outputs of the classification into a graph database for further analysis. I think the first goal is a sufficient enough project to pursue in two weeks.

I initially thought I'd use LlAMP, but that proved difficult because llama is a large model, so I ended up using BLIP as a base and BLIP-2 to finetune.

## Overview

This model was specifically created to provide an optimal text prompt, in different languages. With respect to this project, it helps identify the different clusters of images and image-caption pairs within a food dataset.

*BLIP-2 leverages frozen pre-trained image encoders and large language models (LLMs) by training a lightweight, 12-layer Transformer encoder in between them, achieving state-of-the-art performance on various vision-language tasks. Most notably, BLIP-2 improves upon Flamingo, an 80 billion parameter model, by 8.7% on zero-shot VQAv2 with 54x fewer trainable parameters.* - https://huggingface.co/docs/transformers/en/model_doc/blip-2



## Results

_See [the rubric](https://parsertongue.org/courses/snlp-2/assignments/rubrics/course-project/final/#results)_

Here I looked at the various similarities and losses from the BLIP2 model trained with ViT. I compared the image, text, and multimodal (Image-text pair) embeddings and found that clusters of Image-Images performed best, loss = .55. And output the best similarity scores With pizza and meat plates categories having the highest similarity scores, (.894 and .879)

t-SNE, t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding, shows the diffent modals in a 2-d space and has a 'fuzzy' correlation to the similarity matrix plots (shown in the notebook), where the image embeddings show shapes of loose clusters opposed to the other embedding spaces.

## Error analysis

_See [the rubric](https://parsertongue.org/courses/snlp-2/assignments/rubrics/course-project/final/#error-analysis)_

Contrastive loss was used here to compare the vector similarites using euclidean disctance. The loss function used here was with torch's corss_entropy model.
Images with Images has the smalled loss, .55; multimodal with multimodal had a 1.08 loss; images, and multimodal had a 2.39 loss; and images with text had a 2.96 loss
