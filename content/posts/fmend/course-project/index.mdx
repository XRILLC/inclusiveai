## Project description

_See [the rubric](https://parsertongue.org/courses/snlp-2/assignments/rubrics/course-project/final/#reproducibility).  Use subsections to organize components of the description._

My intention for this project is to evaluate 3 or more LLMs on their ability to finish a story given a short context. I intend on using a dataset
to test against, the ROCStories dataset, which is a dataset comprised of many 5-sentence stories, of which the LLMs are provided with the first
four sentences and asked to complete the story with a final sentence. My intention is to measure BLEU and ROUGE scores, as well as Perplexity. 
I will also be judging each LLM on creativity, but that will be a subjective judgement.

## Error analysis

_See [the rubric](https://parsertongue.org/courses/snlp-2/assignments/rubrics/course-project/final/#error-analysis)_

Taking a look at some of GPT2s errors, which did pretty good in terms of scores:

Semantic Mismatch:

Context: John had always wanted to learn piano. He saved up for months to buy one. When he finally got it home, he was ecstatic. He practiced every day for hours.
Ground Truth: After a year, he could play his favorite song perfectly.
Model Output: The piano gathered dust in the corner of his room.

Logical Inconsistency:

Context: Sarah was preparing for her big presentation. She spent weeks gathering data. The slides were perfect. Her practice runs went smoothly.
Ground Truth: The presentation was a huge success.
Model Output: She decided to cancel the presentation and go swimming instead.

Character Confusion:

Context: Tom and Mike were competing for first place. Tom was ahead by a few points. Mike needed to ace the final test. He studied all night.
Ground Truth: Mike's hard work paid off and he won first place.
Model Output: Tom's studying helped him maintain his lead.


I intentionally shyed away from using the most powerful models for this task for multiple reasons, one being ease and speed (and storage), but also because I knew that some of the errors would be quite funny. For instance, look at some of DistilGPT2s errors:
