## Description
_In your own words, what is this paper about?_

This paper discusses an expirement using Low-Rank Adaption finetuning to develop large language models which can emulate the style of specific authors. Ten authors with large enought bodies of work were chosen and the generations after prompts of pretrained LLMs were compared with finetuned LLMs. Finetuning was done with StyleTunedLM using Low-Rank Adapters with a next token prediction objective. The results showed comparable stylistic matching between pretrained and finetuned models potentially supporting a more efficient method for adapting models to preserve user idiolects. 
The method was evaluated accross linguistic levels: lexical, syntactic, and surface. The results were compared to other traditional methods which also require less computational resources such as few-shot learning and prompt engineering. The StyleTunedLM approach signigicantly outperfromed these methods.
