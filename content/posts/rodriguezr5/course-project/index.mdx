## Project description

The competition dataset consists of user interactions from the ChatBot Arena. 
In each user interaction a judge provides one or more prompts to two different large language models, 
and then indicates which of the models gave the more satisfactory response. The goal of the competition is to predict the preferences 
of the judges and determine the likelihood that a given prompt/response pair is selected as the winner.

_See the [Kaggle competition](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/llm-classification-finetuning/overview.)_

## Project description
### Project Overview
The goal of this project is to predict user preferences in chatbot interactions, focusing on head-to-head comparisons between responses generated by large language models (LLMs). This is achieved by using a BERT-based model to analyze and predict preferences, based on a dataset of labeled interactions.

### Novelty
This project stands out for applying a BERT model to the specialized task of preference prediction in conversational AI. This approach showcases the effectiveness of pre-trained language models in understanding nuanced human judgments, particularly in dialogue scenarios.

### Motivation
The motivation for this project comes from a suggestion by my professor to explore the use of a BERT model for this classification task. Through this experience, I have gained a deeper understanding of complex machine learning models, including how they handle and predict preferences in multi-class scenarios. This project has offered valuable insights into the practical challenges and considerations of implementing state-of-the-art models for real-world applications.

### Challenges of task
- The main challenges of this task include:

- Data Quality: Ensuring that the labels for user preferences are accurate and consistent to avoid introducing errors during training.

- Model Biases: Addressing inherent biases in the models, such as favoring longer responses or the first response displayed, which can skew predictions.

- Scalability: Developing a model that can perform well across a wide range of prompts and scenarios, rather than being specialized to specific types of inputs.

- Evaluation Metrics: Establishing clear and reliable metrics to measure how effectively the model predicts user preferences and aligns with human expectations.

## Proposal for future improvements
### Discussion of limitations
- Data Quality: The set-up can prove difficult due to errors in dataset formatting. 
 
- Dataset Bias: The dataset may focus too heavily on certain types of topics or prompts, which can limit the model's ability to generalize to other kinds of conversations.

- Model Complexity: The current model may struggle to accurately predict more nuanced preferences that require deeper contextual understanding or subtle interpretation.

- Scalability: Limited computational resources restrict the ability to experiment with larger or more complex models that could potentially perform better.

### Avenues for improvement
Exploring Advanced Models: Experiment with multi-modal transformers, which may be better equipped to handle complex and nuanced preferences.

Incorporating Sentiment Analysis: Develop additional evaluation metrics to assess specific aspects of preference alignment, such as capturing the tone or emotional context of a response.
