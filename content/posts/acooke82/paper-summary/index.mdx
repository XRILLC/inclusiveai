## Description

In this paper, the authors discuss how developing AI that have a strong understanding about how humans physically interact with the world is critical for building beneficial AI. While some senses, such as vision and hearing, are already being using in AI applications, smell remains an underdeveloped area of research. The authors propose that LLMs, which have proven themselves capable of making human-like judgements for other senses by conceptualizing them within embedding spaces, could have similar success in the area of smell. The authors set out to test their theory by creating "Sniff AI", a system that analyzes how LLMs encode human scent descriptions and if the embedding spaces created by those descriptions will lead to judgements similar to human ones. The system was split into two tasks, one that focused on if their LLM could correctly guess the scent from a human participant's description and one that focused on if the LLM could correctly identify a second scent based on a human participant's description of how the second scent differed from the first one presented. The study found that the AI was not able to correctly identify the target scent often, with the second task having a marginally better success rate than the first. While the authors expected this result due to humans struggling to conceptualize smell themselves, this suggests that there are significant challenges left to overcome if AI applications are looking to include impactful scent-based experiences.
