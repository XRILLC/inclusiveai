#### Description (LLM)

The paper discusses several vulnerabilities in machine learning models that rely on web-scale datasets, including poisoning attacks such as frontrunning and common crawl poisoning. The authors argue that these attacks can be carried out today and should be addressed to improve the trustworthiness of web-scale data. They propose implementing various defenses, such as integrity checks, randomized or time-gated snapshots, and binary transparency, to mitigate these vulnerabilities.

#### What are the authors proposing?
The authors of the paper are proposing several solutions to address the poisoning attacks on web-scale datasets:

1. Implementing integrity checks: Regularly checking data for consistency before training models can help prevent poisoning.

2. Randomized or time-gated snapshots: Randomizing the snapshot order of articles or using a longer retention period to increase the likelihood that an attacker will fail to modify a particular article.

3. Cherry-picking modifications: Creating an initial snapshot of an article, holding it for a period T > ∆, and then back-applying modifications from trusted moderators.

4. Binary transparency: Including all modules in a dataset in binary form (e.g., including only the necessary code) to prevent selective inclusion of malicious modules.

These proposals aim to provide a more robust approach to mitigating poisoning attacks on web-scale datasets, which are critical for training and testing various AI and NLP tasks.
