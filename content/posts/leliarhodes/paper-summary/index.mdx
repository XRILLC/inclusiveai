## Description

This paper seeks to explore and expand upon our current understanding of Natural Language Processing in regards to signed languages (specifically American Sign Language) in order to create greater accessibility for D/deaf people in the field of AI utilization. The focus of this paper is on fingerspelling and the recognition of such words. Fingerspelling is the act of using a signed alphabet to spell written words, typically used in the case of names, proper nouns, and words that do not have a direct sign correlation. There are also places in the US that utilize more heavy fingerspelling, such as in the case of The Rochester Method which is almost exclusively comprised of fingerspelling, as opposed to traditional signs. This paper seeks to further our understanding of technology and provide further access for those who cannot/do not use spoken language.

## Motivation

Throughout this program, we have focused exclusively on spoken languages, which is to be expected. I wanted to branch out and see how NLP can be used in the case of signed languages, which is more my area of expertise. I felt that beggining with the basics would be beneficial as a good grounding point before moving on to more advanced topics. Fingerspelling utilizes only 30-ish handshapes/positions (1 for each letter, along with accents/dialectic differences), allowing for a smaller required base for the machine to comprehend the target word/letter.
