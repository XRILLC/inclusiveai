## Description

- The authors of this paper present an NLP approach to help diagnose Alzheimer’s Disease by analyzing language patterns linked to the condition. They use a combination of CNN and LSTM to capture both local and global textual features from patient speech data, specifically from the DementiaBank's Pitt corpus. TextCNN extracts short-term, local text features, while LSTM captures long-term dependencies in the language, effectively identifying patterns associated with Alzheimer’s Disease.  This model leverages BERT embeddings for initial word representation and combines CNN and LSTM outputs to enhance classification accuracy. Their model achieves a competitive accuracy of 89.3%, outperforming other traditional machine learning and deep learning models in Alzheimer’s Disease detection, making it a promising tool for diagnosis.

## Motivation

- I chose this topic because I'm fascinated by how NLP can be used to improve people's lives, particularly when it comes to Alzheimer's disease. By analyzing speech patterns, NLP can help patients and their families detect early signs of Alzheimer's, which can lead to earlier diagnosis and intervention. This is especially important because Alzheimer's is a condition that progresses slowly and identifying it early can help families take preventive measures and manage care more effectively.
