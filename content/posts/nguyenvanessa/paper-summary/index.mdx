## Description

This paper examines how a new model, MBERT-TextRCNN-PL, can be used to identify emotions during Hindi-English conversations. It combines prompt learning with MBERT-TextRCNN to improve accuracy. The paper also suggests an emotion capturer model, XGBoost-EC, to identify what causes changes in emotions during both Hindi-English conversations and English-only conversations. Both models' F1 scores outperformed those of comparable models like BERT and DialogXL.

## Motivation

I chose this paper because I was interested in how models can be modified to improve emotion recognition when even humans can find it difficult to recognize and convey the right tone through text.

The approach or innovation proposed in this paper is the development of models for emotion recognition and emotion flipping reasoning in dialogues.

For Emotion Recognition (ERC) task, the authors propose the MBERT-TextRCNN-PL model, which utilizes a prompt learning approach to contextualize the conversation. This model combines the features of MBERT and TextRCNN to better understand the conversation history and context. The model is trained using the Hugging Face Transformers library, and F1 score is used as the performance metric.

For Emotion Flipping Reasoning (EFR) task, the authors propose the XGBoost-EC model, which is based on XGBoost and designed to utilize emotion feature encoding for training and prediction. This model encodes emotion labels to integer values and assigns -1 for missing emotions. The model uses emotion windows for encoding, which allows it to learn the associations between emotions and other features and predict emotional states in text or dialogue more accurately.

Additionally, for the EFR task, the authors propose a method for identifying emotional instigators, the TGIF framework, which utilizes a combination of Transformer and GRU to identify emotional instigators in multilingual dialogue data.

The authors validate their models using the EDiReF shared task at SemEval 2024, and the results show that their models are effective in recognizing emotions and identifying emotional instigators in dialogues.
