## Project description

This is obviously a rough draft, but at a high level, I want to see how well I can use LLMs to create synthetic training data for creating some common NLP tools 
like NER or POS tagging.  The idea being that I will explore some smaller available models like llama3.2, phi3.5 and others to leverage the ability of 
said LLMs to recognize named entities and parts of speech and use this as the basis for training a simpler model.

A rought outline of what I am going to do is as follows:

- Curate training data for NER and/or POS generated by the LLMs, which should in theory be good (?) at doing these tasks 
- Explore creating NN classifier using pytorch to strengthen my understanding with creating deep models
- Compare my model against some standard benchmarks.
  - Further compare how the various source LLMs, some techniques for generating the synthetic training data go
  - Compare versus training my classifiers on any open human tagged data sets

I think the outcome of this would be interesting as it will get me to explore some of the new ML techniques I am 
learning in this class, get some exposure to the current trend of LLMs for everything, and also reinforce some 
work with "classic" problems.  My overarching theme for the project will be to see if I can leverage 
the huge amount of knowledge in LLMs (even if maybe I use small ones in this exercise) to extract out efficient 
models that in some sense already leverage all of the training that went into them to creat smaller more fine-grained
models that can perfom efficiently without having to use human effort in tagging.  There seems to perhaps be some 
pratical application as having models that can perform certain subets of NLP work on less powerful devices would have 
a real-world use.  From an educational standpoint, I will be able to touch on various ares of NLP, leaning new 
things while reinforcing current learnings on NNs and old material like basic NLP tasks. I may find out that this 
just blows up in my face, but I think there will be some definite knowledge to be gained

Putting things more formally:

## Goals
- Explore the data generation capabilities, in particular for NLP tasks (either or both NER and POS) of various accessible LLMs
- Build a NER and/or POS tagger using deep learning techniques to reinforce using these techniques
- Benchmark how well or not well this approach works against and standard benchmarks
- Reflect on my results or lack thereof and what they may say about the LLMs, my classifiers and general ideas
