## Description

The paper goes over a technique for updating a deep network's weight Mass-Editing Memory in a Transformer or MEMIT,
which aims to solve the problem that large scale models become obsolete and need to be updating without fully retraining.
The author's propose MEMIT as a way to efficiently bulk adds new memories to an existing model
and provides performance benefits over other knowledge editing techniques.  The paper provides 
the MEMIT Algorithm and an analysis of its performance on two LLMs GPT-J (6B) and GPT-NeoX (20B) 
compared against other knowledge editing algorithms: FT-W, MEND, and ROME.

## Motivation

I originally found this while exploring papers where one explored the efficacy on knowledge editing for correcting 
hallucinations, which in turn led me through sifting through a few more papers before settling on this particular paper.
The exploration of how to keep models up to date efficiently and the somewhat recentness of this paper made me 
feel it was worth exploring as it dives deep into one technique.  While I do not think I will quickly grasp all the 
material, it seems like a paper that has utility and will expose me to some important pratical ideas for an obvious 
problem.  In some sense I see this as how can one go from development to maintenance mode on an LLM.  Further 
some of the implications for how to "patch" a model for safety and accuracy considerations without throwing out 
the whole thing seem to be ideas of high utility.  In short, I think this paper will really challenge me and 
expose me to some things and will be one I may want to continue to go back to later as my knowledge expands 
from my current place as both this class and my continued learning progress.
