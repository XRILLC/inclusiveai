## Description

- This paper introduces TelME, a new approach to Emotion Recognition in Conversation (ERC) that improves emotion detection by combining text, audio and visual data. Traditional methods focus mainly on text, neglecting important non-verbal cues like tone of voice and facial expressions. TelME solves this by using cross-modal knowledge distillation(KD), where a strong text model (the teacher) helps improve the weaker audio and visual models (the students). Additionally, it uses a unique technique to combine the features from all three data types, ensuring the system can better recognize emotions from different sources. Through experiments on the MELD and IEMOCAP datasets, TelME performs better than other methods, especially in conversations with multiple speakers, offering a promising new way to enhance emotion recognition systems.

## Motivation

- I chose this paper because it presents an innovative approach to Emotion Recognition in Conversation (ERC) using multimodal data, which is crucial for developing more responsive and human-like dialogue systems. Traditional ERC systems often focus on text-based analysis, not focusing on non-verbal cues like facial expressions and voice tone, which are important for fully understanding emotions. The paper introduces TelME, a new framework that strengthens weaker modalities through cross-modal knowledge distillation, especially in multi-party conversations. This research is particularly relevant as emotion-aware systems become increasingly important in fields like customer service, healthcare and social robots, offering a promising solution to improving emotion recognition and enhancing interactions in real-world settings.
